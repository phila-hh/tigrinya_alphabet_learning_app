Tigrinya Alphabet

Tigrinya, a Semitic (Afro-Asiatic) language, is part of a linguistic lineage that includes modern languages such as Tigrinya, Tigre, Amharic, Hebrew, Arabic, Maltese, and Aramaic, all stemming from the Biblical "Shem." Spoken by approximately 10 million people worldwide, Tigrinya holds significant cultural importance in Tigray, the northern region of Ethiopia, and parts of Eritea.

Written in the Ge'ez script, originally crafted for the now-extinct Ge'ez language, Tigrinya employs a system of 32 characters. Although the Ge'ez script may seem daunting at first glance, mastering it to read Tigrinya proves relatively straightforward. Pronunciation follows a simple and intuitive rule: "You write as you read it, and you read as you write it." With its left-to-right orientation, akin to English, Tigrinya offers an accessible entry point for learners eager to explore its linguistic richness.
